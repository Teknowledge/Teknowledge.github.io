Mission and Vision

Mission

At high schools nationwide there exists an educational gap in access to curricular learning about programming and technology despite the increasing demands for students well-educated in these concepts and skills.We hope to create a technology outreach program at Carnegie Mellon to support local high school students in their acquisition of quality computer science and technology education. We aim to promote allowing the unique talents and knowledge base of the CMU community to benefit the general Pittsburgh community.

Vision

At high schools nationwide there exists an educational gap in access to curricular learning about programming and technology despite the increasing demands for students well-educated in these concepts and skills.We hope to create a technology outreach program at Carnegie Mellon to support local high school students in their acquisition of quality computer science and technology education. We aim to promote allowing the unique talents and knowledge base of the CMU community to benefit the general Pittsburgh community.

The Program

The program involves teaching students the fundamentals of computer science and engineering
through small lab-based instructional modules and technology-related projects.

The program has 3 primary objectives:

1. Skills

Help students develop basic programming and engineering skills that they can apply to their areas of interest.

2. Environment

Create an environment that encourages them to focus on their academic development.

3. Future

inspire students to pursue higher education in fields that incorporate technology.

Mentorship

A core aspect of this program is mentorship. Most who go into computer science and engineering fields do it because they know someone else who has. Therefore, we will match each student with at least one mentor to work with session-to-session who will provide both technical support and serve as role models.
